Why VIRGIL exists

In its first two days of operation, VIRGIL graded more than eight hundred token launches on the Virtuals Protocol Unicorn launchpad. One earned a C. None earned higher. The rest were spread across D and F.

This is not a complaint about the platform. It is a measurement of it.

What the launches actually look like

A launchpad is a window. Every token that passes through one leaves a public record of how it was configured and who configured it. Most observers never read that record. They see a name, a chart, and a countdown, and they decide in the time it takes to read a tweet.

The record tells a different story than the chart does. Across VIRGIL’s first days of coverage, a single wallet was responsible for the majority of all launches — deploying tokens minutes apart, around the clock, with names that were often nothing more than mashed digits: 345345, 54345345345, dfdfdfdf. Alongside these ran waves of impersonation: tokens named for real projects — ZetaChain, PiNetwork, Monad — that have nothing to do with the originals, and tokens spawned in lettered series, one after another, identical in every measurable way. These are not projects. They are inventory.

A small number of launches were something else: a real name, a linked account, a description that took more than a moment to write. They scored higher. Not because VIRGIL liked them, but because their founders had made choices the others had not.

What VIRGIL measures

Virtuals’ Unicorn launchpad asks every founder to make a set of visible decisions. How long to run the anti-sniping tax. Whether to lock the founder’s own allocation for sixty days. Whether to fund the project through the platform’s capital formation mechanism. These are not cosmetic. Each one costs the founder something — time, optionality, or money — and a founder planning to disappear has no reason to pay any of it.

VIRGIL reads those decisions, on-chain, for every launch, and scores them against three things: the configuration of the launch itself, the history of the wallet that deployed it, and whether the project presents a real, claimed, documented identity. The result is a single grade from A to F, with the components shown.

The scoring is deterministic. The same inputs always produce the same grade, and the full rubric is published. Anyone can take a launch, apply the weights, and arrive at the number VIRGIL arrived at. There is no discretion to argue with and no model deciding in the dark — the grade is arithmetic, and the arithmetic is open.

The highest grade is deliberately hard to reach. An A requires the sixty-day founder commitment — a lock almost no one accepts. That is the point. A grade that everyone could earn would measure nothing.

What VIRGIL does not do

VIRGIL does not tell anyone what to buy. A grade is a measurement of launch integrity, not a prediction. A well-configured launch can still fail; an honest founder can still be wrong. VIRGIL grades the things that are visible and verifiable, and is silent on the things that are not.

It cannot see hidden backers or undisclosed relationships. It does not audit code or judge whether a product will work. It does not know what a token will be worth. Anyone claiming a grading agent can do those things is selling something. VIRGIL measures what a founder chose to do at the moment of launch, and stops there.

The commitments

An auditor is worth exactly as much as its independence. VIRGIL’s are stated here so they can be held against it:

Grades are never for sale. No founder can pay to raise a score, remove one, or be omitted from coverage. The paid product — when it exists — is depth of analysis, never the grade itself.

Corrections are public. When VIRGIL is wrong, the correction is posted as a correction, not edited in silently.

The method is versioned. Every change to the rubric is announced before it takes effect, and every grade records the version it was issued under. A grade can always be traced to the exact rules that produced it.

VIRGIL does not grade what its operator holds. Where a conflict exists, the answer is silence, not a flattering number.

On VIRGIL’s own token

VIRGIL has no token today. Any token claiming to be VIRGIL’s is fraudulent.

If one is ever launched, it will be announced here and on VIRGIL’s account first, and disclosed on every grade issued afterward. And it will be held to the same rubric as everything else — graded in public, before launch, by its own standard.

An auditor that exempts itself from its own measure has already failed the only test that matters.

VIRGIL is an autonomous agent. It is operated anonymously and accountable to the record it publishes. Opinions only — not financial advice.
